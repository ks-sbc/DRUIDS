    Frequently Asked Questions¶

    General Questions¶

    Why not just use Google Docs?¶

    Would you hand over your organizations most sensitive documents to a federal agent? Probably not. Well, when you use Google Docs, that is exactly what you are doing whether or not you intend to do so! Comrade, but isn't it true that Microsoft runs GitHub? Yes, that is a true statement, but a few points to consider:

    1. Does your organization have a public website? If so, you already gave every single thing on that website to the FBI. Guaranteed, 100% of the time, every single time. And as will be discussed in our Security Theory, most of the stuff you should be doing at this point of development should be public! After all, how else are the masses supposed to find you if the only place you hang out is the dark web? But for those odd 5-10% of documents that are not public information, well - we use RSA encryption to keep them safe. Not even the feds can break into that.

    Is this too complex for small organizations?¶

    No! DRUIDS is designed for 5-50 person organizations. The 15-minute quick start gets you running fast, and the visual security indicators prevent mistakes. It's actually simpler than managing scattered Google Docs, Discord channels, and Signal threads.

    What about non-technical members?¶

    Most DRUIDS usage requires no technical skills:

    • Creating meeting minutes is template-based
    • Security is visual (green/gold/red borders)
    • Search is just Ctrl+O
    • Everything works like a wiki

    The technical setup is one-time, then everyone can contribute.

    Security Questions¶

    How secure is it really?¶

    DRUIDS implements real security for real threats:

    • L2 isolation protects against raids and subpoenas
    • Git distribution prevents single points of failure
    • Visual indicators prevent accidental leaks
    • Offline capability works without internet

    It's not paranoia - it's protection against documented threats to organizers.

    What if someone's device is compromised?¶

    The three-tier system limits damage:

    • L0 (public) exposure doesn't matter
    • L1 (member) exposure is contained
    • L2 (cadre) is isolated on separate devices/repos

    Plus Git history shows exactly what was accessed when.

    Implementation Questions¶

    What's the real time investment?¶

    • 15 minutes: See value with quick start
    • 45 minutes: Full local setup
    • 2-3 days: Organization fully onboarded
    • Ongoing: 5 minutes per meeting for minutes

    Compare to hours lost searching chat history or recreating lost documents.

    Can we migrate existing content?¶

    Yes! The Migration Guide covers this. In a nutshell, you simply export your Google Docs into the Markdown format, and then import them into DRUIDS. Don't worry about if they're nice and tidy - we have automatic cleaning codes that will take care of all that for you!

    If you can get it in a markdown file, you can get it into DRUIDS.

    What if our main organizer leaves?¶

    That's exactly what DRUIDS prevents! With distributed Git repos:

    • No single person has all passwords
    • Knowledge is preserved in Git history
    • New people can onboard from documentation
    • Organization continues without disruption

    Organizational Questions¶

    How does this help with democratic centralism?¶

    DRUIDS embeds democratic centralist principles into the technology. The centralism comes from having centrally enforced procedures and workflows that are transparently and equally enforced. The democracy comes from being assured by a cryptotgraphic algorithm that the decisions are made by the majority of the organization, and that the decisions are made by the people who are most knowledgeable about the issue at hand.

    Will this create more work for our secretary?¶

    Actually less! DRUIDS automates:

    • Document ID generation
    • Meeting minute templates
    • Security classification
    • Task extraction from meetings
    • Searchable archives

    No more maintaining multiple Google Docs or losing notes in Signal.

    How do we handle conflicts or disagreements?¶

    Git's merge conflict resolution mirrors organizational conflict resolution:

    • Different views are explicitly shown
    • Must be resolved through discussion
    • Final decision is recorded
    • Can always see what changed and why

    What about organizing actions or protests?¶

    L2 security tier is designed for sensitive planning:

    • Completely isolated from public/member tiers
    • Not accessible even if devices are seized
    • Visual indicators prevent accidental exposure
    • Can still coordinate without phone metadata trails

    Practical Concerns¶

    Do we need to be online all the time?¶

    No! DRUIDS works fully offline5:

    • Obsidian is a local application
    • Git repos are on your machine
    • Sync when you have internet
    • Perfect for rural organizing

    What if we're not "tech people"?¶

    Most revolutionary orgs aren't! DRUIDS uses:

    • Simple markdown (like texting)
    • Visual cues instead of complex permissions
    • Templates for everything
    • Community support available

    Tech knowledge helps but isn't required for daily use.

    How much does this cost?¶

    Absolutely nothing except your time and attention. There are no bills or subscriptions to worry about! DRUIDS is built on free/open source tools:

    • Git: Free
    • Obsidian: Free for personal use
    • GitHub: Free for public repos
    • MkDocs: Free
    • Your time: Invaluable but well-spent

    However, if you would like to support the development of DRUIDS, you can make a donation to the Cadre of KSBC who have been working on it for months, putting in countless hours of work.

    Can we customize it for our organization?¶

    Yes, if you are feeling up to the task of developing your own solution, you are free to do so! As long as you keep the software open source, you credit the Kansas Socialist Book Club for developing the original copy, and you DO NOT make ANY monetary profits from it, then what you do with DRUIDS is your business.

    However, if you find something not to your liking, the developers strongly encourage you to reach out and let them know what kind of improvements you had in mind. Teamwork makes the dream work, after all!

    What if Git/GitHub goes away?¶

    Right now, most orgs who will consider DRUIDS are probably small, local book clubs or mutual aid societies well-versed in Marxism. Some might call our product overkill, but we prefer to call it scalability. DRUIDS is, in fact, desinged to be capable of expansion. It's also designed to be portable, and not bound to any one platform.1 Your data is never locked in for the following reasons:

    1. All content is plain text. You can literally use any word processor you want, even the ancient vi or notepad.
    2. Git, the underlying technology behind the merging capabilities, is 100% free and open source. It was invented by Linus Torvalds, the same guy who invented the Linux kernel. Git repos can move to any Git host.2
    3. We prefer you to use it on our custom image of the Tails operating system, which will eventually be conveniently packaged for you to install yourself3 on a thumb drive. However, you could operate it on any operating system4.

    Is this just for socialists?¶

    While we know there are certain fascists who take a keen interest in projects like DRUIDS, it' snot because they want to use it themselves! However, we suppose there is absolutely nothing stopping the Federal Bureau of Investigations4, Turning Point USA or Peter Thiel from appropriating DRUIDS for their own methods. Other than the fact that fascists are firstly often working in tech thesmselves, and two that they have boatloads more money than we do.

    Getting Started¶

    Where do we start?¶

    1. One person does the Quick Start (15 minutes)
    2. Show others at your next meeting
    3. Pilot with meeting minutes for a month
    4. Leave A Comment on the Giscus with any questions, issues, problems, or suggestions you have. We love comradely criticism!

    Don't try to migrate everything at once.

    Who should champion this in our org?¶

    Ideal DRUIDS champion:

    • Frustrated with current chaos
    • Basic computer comfort (uses Google Docs)
    • Respected by other members
    • Committed to organizational development

    Not necessarily your most technical person!

    What if people resist the change?¶

    Common resistance and solutions:

    • "Too complicated" Start with just meeting minutes
    • "We're fine now" Show search capabilities
    • "Security theater" Discuss real threats faced
    • "More work" Demonstrate time saved

    Let results speak for themselves.
