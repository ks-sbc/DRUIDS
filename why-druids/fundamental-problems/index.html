    What is DRUIDS?¶

    Before we can answer what DRUIDS is, we should answer a more fundamental question: what do organizations actually do? Political organizations are composed of people, but they're more than the sum of the individual members. An organization is a finely-tuned cybernetic system, operated and shaped by the people within it. Therefore, an organization performs some kind of a function. And what exactly is a function? A function is fundamentally a kind of relationship that takes something as an input, transforms it, and then returns it as output which in turn is fed into the next iteration of the function. So, since organizations have some manner of function, DRUIDS can be said to be a framework for building and operating organizations that allows them to precisely define and execute their function.

    Fundamental Problems of Organizations¶

    In our experience, we have determined the following problems to be the most fundamental problems from which all organizations suffer in the United States. These problems are not unique to the United States, but they are particularly acute here. We want to address these problems not just by pointing themn out, but instead by providing a solution. This is our attempt to do precisely that. The six problems are:

    Political Errors¶

    Organizations often hold incorrect political lines that don't correspond to material reality. They make analyses based on idealistic conceptions of the world, rather than dealing with the world as it actually exists. This is a problem because it leads to a lack of clarity about what the organization is actually doing, and it leads to a lack of clarity about what the organization is actually trying to achieve.

    DRUIDS cannot determine the corrrect political line; only human beings can do that. What DRUIDS can do, however, is provide a framework for developing and refining political understanding by giving you a secure and structured way to organize your work, your documents, and your decisions. They are organized and sturctured in such a way that democratic centralism is hard-coded into the very technological structure of DRUIDS.

    DRUIDS has a focus on privacy, and the only data that is shared with others is the data that your organization has explicitly chosen to share.

    Inability to Reproduce Cadres¶

    Have you ever been in an organization where it all revolves around one really enthusiastic, hard-working person with whom all of the knowledge, the experience, and the skills lie with? KSBC has dealt with precisely that scenario, and it is a dire one that affects many organizations. In the best possibl case, that cadre will simply get burnt out and leave. In the worst case, that cadre becomes a charismatic leader like Mussolini and the organization degenerates into a cult.

    The fundamental cause of this problem is that organizations do not have a formal mechanism or process for reproducing their cadres. An org runs on the labor power of its members, and if the burden of that labor power is concentrated on a select few, then the organization will be unable to function. No matter how well-intentioned the cadre might be, this state of afffairs will inevitably be anti-democratic.

    DRUIDS cannot reproduce cadres. It is a piece of software, and it cannot do what a human being can do. However, DRUIDS can provide a framework for reproducing cadres by allowing you to: - Document the ideological growth of your membership - Track membership skills and experience - Ensure that the burden of labor power is fairly and equally distributed - Identify trends from data to give insight into recruitment efforts

    No Institutional Memory¶

    Organizations often operate on "vibes" rather than systematic processes. Because the analysis is not centered around facts acquired through scientific inquiry and social investigations, the organization is unable to make rational decisions based on an objective understanding of the material conditions of the world. Therefore, decisions made at meetings never get implemented; action items lack owners and timelines; lessons learned are forgotten; and best practices aren't documented.

    This creates a number of weaknesses in the organization. Firstly, it creates a breeding ground for the tendency of charismatic leadership because if there is no institutional memory, then everything falls on the shoulders of one guy. And if one guy has all of the information? All of the passwords, the experience, the knowledge, etc? That's just begging to create a Mussolini! Secondly, it's a direct contributor to burnout. All of the burdens and pressures falling on one persons shoulder is exhausting. Finally, it is the principal reason why orgs keep doing the same stuff over and over again but expecting different results.

    DRUIDS cannot do everything in and of tiself, but one thing it can do and does very well, it to be your institutions memory. Let DRUIDS take th eburden off your shoulders. We will be your secure repository, your hard drive for bureaucracy.

    Security Errors¶

    A common error made by many organizations is that they uphold what's known as "Security Theater". This is basically the error of "going through the motions" not because you understand them fully, but because that's just the way things have always been done and its whate veryone else is doing anyways. Security theater is about the illusion of feeling safe; it is an idealistic and bourgeois notion that centers around individual subjectivities. True security, however, is proletarian because it is a reproducible process based on scientific evidence and best practices. It is not a matter of feeling safe, but of being safe. Examples of security theater include:

    • Using an "encrypted" app without having actually investigated the esourcde code
    • Downloading the Signal app you use for sensitive communications from the Google Play Store.
    • Refusing to use an effective product because "a corporation made it" or because "it's fed technology" without realizing that those are actually the best indicators that it works!

    Operational Security - or OPSEC - is another major mistake made by many orgs. By OPSEC we mean the process through which critical information is identified, and steps are taken to prevent adversarial forces (fascists, repressive police state) from accessing it. Closely related to OPSEC is the concept of Compartmentalization, which is the process of separating different types of information and work to limit exposure if security is breached.

    Imagine you're in an organization, working on your personal computer on some sensitive political business. You download a secret document onto your personal computer. There's no separation! This is horribl esecurity practice. DRUIDS however, makes it quite litearlly impossible because the classifications of security levels - IE compartmentalization - is enforced technologically through the git repositories and branch structures. Assuming that permissions are assigned correctly, it is quite literally impossible to not have compartmentalization because it is baked into the structure of DRUIDS.1

    Finally there is a complete lack of threat modeling. What little threat modeling exists, boils down to the vibes and feelings of individual members in the group. While we understand that sometimes peoples intuitions are correct, and often times the correct ones are formed through unfortunate experiences, it is equally true that just as often - if not moreso - that someones perceptions or intutions are wrong. The methods are not reproducible, and the results aren't verifiable. What organizations need especially in this era of ascendant and revanchist fascism is a systematic and reproducible process for identifying threats and taking steps to mitigate them. Security is best when it's proactive, not reactive. And DRUIDS, through its documentation system, can help you do just that.

    Think of this real scenario2 that occurs all too often: some man is a known predator, and after he assaults someone in an org, he is evicted. But he simply moves to another one and does the same thing all over again because nobody documents anything, nobody keeps files or records, and therefore nobody can be said to have any sort of memory. This is a problem because it is a clear case of a failure to identify and mitigate threats. But what if you were using a system that made it super easy to document, track, and identify threats? THen not only could you prevent this all-too-common scenario from occuring, but you can furthermore share that information with other organizations to keep the community safe. Wouldn't that be great? Well it's possible with DRUIDS.

    Scatterbrained Reports¶

    Have you ever had well-intentioned and eager members attend some sort of demonstration or event, but then when they back, they can't remember anything for the life of them? That's probably because they didn't document anything! And even if they did document something, then there's still the issue of dissemination. Where do comrades send their reports to and know that they will be secure and safe, their information protected? How do comrades know what kind of information is worth recording, and what is irrelveant or not useful?

    DRUIDS is a perfect solution to these problems. Our security protocols are designed to keep things safe with hardware-based encryption, as well as the other security protocols discussed above. You can archive your data knowing it will be safe, and WILL NOT be seen by anybody unless you choose to share it with them proactively. That includes us as well! KSBC doesn't retain any ownership of your data, we don't receieve anything unless you want to share with us. DRUIDS also has a number of useful templates for reports, which completely solves the issue of how to report on events.

    Key Benefits¶

    • Preserve institutional knowledge
    • Scale effectively to new chapters
    • Maintain security under repression
    • Make decisions democratically
    • Track progress objectively
